B.A. in Liberal Arts and Sciences

University of Illinois, Urbana-Champaign, 2004

Double Major in English and Rhetoric

While enrolled, I was an Illinois State Scholar and Dean's List recipient who maintained a 3.5 major GPA and participated in an innovative research project under Dr. Nancy Ablemann, the Ethnography of the University project. I conducted instructor supervised, IRB approved research into the 2003 budget cuts at UIUC and my research and feedback on the project is archived and referenced in pedagogical initiatives currently underway at the university.

M.A. in English and American Literature

DePaul University, Chicago, 2008

I conducted research of technical detail in fiction (e.g. how authors create believability in fiction through the inclusion of technical detail), specifically how authors incorporated details of socioeconomic and class differences to engage period audiences in fiction and drama. I graduated with distinction (3.95 GPA), accumulating an wide variety of experience in research throughout my program.

Ph.D. Candidate in Technical Communication

Illinois Institute of Technology, Chicago, 2009-Present

As a student, I am working on research into increasing technological proficiencies in the United States citizenry and advocating for an increase in awareness of the need to efficiently and persuasively communicate information to decision makers and laypersons with regard to public policy issues. As a writing center tutor and an adjunct faculty member teaching in Humanities and English, I am working to improve the visibility and relevance of an education in the humanities at a technological university.
